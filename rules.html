How to Play

🎮 Basic Objective
Tic Tac Toe is a classic two-player game played on a 3×3 grid. The goal is to get three of your symbols (X or O) in a row—either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally—before your opponent does.
📋 Game Rules
  • Turn-based gameplay: Players take turns placing their symbol (X or O) on an empty cell of the grid.
  • First player starts: X always goes first.
  • One move per turn: Each player places exactly one symbol per turn.
  • Win condition: Get three of your symbols in a row (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) to win immediately.
  • Draw: If all 9 cells are filled and neither player has three in a row, the game ends in a draw.
  • Game over: Once a win or draw is detected, no more moves can be made.
👥 Game Modes

Local Multiplayer

Play with a friend on the same device. Two players take turns clicking cells on the grid. Perfect for casual gaming with someone nearby.

Computer (3 Difficulty Levels)

Easy: The computer makes random valid moves. Great for beginners learning the game.
Medium: The computer plays strategically—it will take winning moves when available and block your winning moves. A balanced challenge.
Hard: The computer uses the Minimax algorithm, making it mathematically unbeatable. It will never lose. Only possible outcomes are Computer Win or Draw.

Online Multiplayer

Play with friends remotely using room codes. One player hosts a game and receives a unique room code. The opponent opens the game in another browser tab/window, joins with the room code, and the game syncs in real-time. Built-in chat lets you communicate during the game.
⚡ Winning Strategies
  • Control the center: The middle cell (5) is part of 4 winning lines. Controlling it gives you more winning opportunities.
  • Watch for threats: If your opponent has two in a row, block the third cell immediately or they'll win.
  • Create forks: Try to position yourself so you have two possible winning moves. Your opponent can only block one.
  • Corners are powerful: Corner cells (1, 3, 7, 9) are part of 3 winning lines each. They're strategically valuable.
  • Play optimally: With perfect play, Tic Tac Toe always ends in a draw. Focus on not making mistakes against skilled opponents.
